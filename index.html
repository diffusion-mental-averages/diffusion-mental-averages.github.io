Abstract

Can a diffusion model produce its own “mental average” of a concept—one that is as sharp and realistic as a typical sample? We introduce Diffusion Mental Averages (DMA), a model-centric answer to this question. While prior methods aim to average image collections, they produce blurry results when applied to diffusion samples from the same prompt. These data-centric techniques operate outside the model, ignoring the generative process. In contrast, DMA averages within the diffusion model’s semantic space, as discovered by recent studies. Since this space evolves across timesteps and lacks a direct decoder, we cast averaging as trajectory alignment: optimize multiple noise latents so their denoising trajectories progressively converge toward shared coarse-to-fine semantics, yielding a single sharp prototype. We extend our approach to multimodal concepts (e.g., dogs with many breeds) by clustering samples in semantically-rich spaces such as CLIP and applying Textual Inversion or LoRA to bridge CLIP clusters into diffusion space. This is, to our knowledge, the first approach that delivers consistent, realistic averages, even for abstract concepts, serving as a concrete visual summary and a lens into model biases and concept representation.

Approach

Given a concept prompt, our goal is to synthesize an average image that captures its shared semantics under a probe diffusion model while preserving visual realism.

Because diffusion models distribute semantic information—from coarse structures to fine details—along their denoising trajectory, simply averaging in the image space or a single latent layer is ineffective. To solve this, we align multiple denoising trajectories so they converge toward a shared semantic consensus.

Our key idea is to jointly optimize multiple noise latents through the following process:
1. Average: At each timestep, we average their h-space activations to form a shared semantic target.
2. Optimize: We optimize each latent to match this target before denoising to the next step.
3. Repeat: We repeat this across all timesteps to align the coarse-to-fine semantics, yielding a single "mental average" of the concept.

Mode Averages

A single average may oversimplify concepts that have several distinct semantic modes. For example, “crane” can refer to either a bird or a construction machine, which are two modes that are difficult to meaningfully blend into a single prototype. To address this, we extend our method by first clustering samples in semantically rich spaces, such as OpenAI CLIP (unsupervised) or BLIP-VQA (grounded). Before averaging, we bridge the gap between the clustering space and the diffusion latent space using techniques such as Textual Inversion or LoRA to steer the denoising process toward each cluster’s semantic subregion. Finally, we apply our Diffusion Mental Average method to generate a separate mode-specific average for each cluster.
